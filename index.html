The Starting Point

Lean base, clear target

BMI ~19.3 — nothing to lose, everything to build. The plan is pure muscle and skill construction, not fat loss.

26 years old, 169cm, 55kg, athletic build. Living in a dormitory in İzmir with no cooking access — every meal on this plan comes ready-to-eat from the market.

The goal isn't just size. It's a physique that's strong and loose — full range of motion, good posture, no stiffness. That's why this plan blends calisthenics (control, mobility, skill) with weights (targeted, measurable overload).

  • Build to 68-70kg
    Slow lean gain, roughly 0.3-0.5kg per week
  • Full-range strength
    Pull-ups, dips, squats, presses — bodyweight and loaded
  • Stay flexible
    Mandatory stretching every session, no exceptions
  • No-cook fuel
    Everything sourced from İzmir markets, zero stove needed

Before Training (4-7 PM Slot)

  • 60-90 min before: carb + protein snack — banana with peanut butter, or yogurt with honey and oats.
  • Hydrate: a full glass of water when you leave the lab, another before you start lifting.
  • Optional: tea or coffee 30-45 min before if you want an energy lift after a long lab day.
  • Avoid: a heavy, high-fat meal right before — it sits in the stomach and drags energy down during lifts.
  • At the gym: 5-8 min dynamic warm-up (see Cardio section) before touching a bar.

Nutrition Rules
  • Eat on schedule: don't skip a meal or snack — this is how the surplus stays consistent.
  • Protein first: hit protein at every meal before worrying about anything else.
  • Weigh in weekly: same day, same time — track the trend, not the daily noise.
  • No sugary drinks: soda and sweetened tea don't count toward the surplus you want.
  • Kettle access? boiled eggs become an easy, cheap add — check with the dorm.
  • Big jump on the scale? that's mostly water or fat — slow and steady is the target.
Conditioning

Cardio & Warm-Up Protocol

Your goal is muscle gain, not fat loss — so cardio here is kept light and purposeful: enough for heart health and conditioning, not so much that it eats into your calorie surplus.

Warm-Up Cardio (Every Training Day)

Always comes first, before any lifting or calisthenics. Raises core temperature and heart rate so joints and muscles are ready to load.

  • MethodBrisk treadmill walk / jog or jump rope
  • IntensityLight — able to talk
  • Duration5-8 minutes
  • Target Heart Rate~100-120 bpm
  • Followed ByDynamic stretches (arm circles, leg swings, hip openers, cat-cow)

Optional Conditioning (1-2 Off Days)

Light LISS cardio on a non-lifting day — good for heart health and recovery blood flow, without interfering with muscle building. Skip it on weeks where recovery feels behind.

  • MethodWalking, light jog, or cycling
  • IntensityEasy, conversational pace
  • Duration20-25 minutes
  • Target Heart Rate110-130 bpm
  • Frequency1-2x per week, max
Keep off-day cardio short and easy. Long or intense cardio sessions burn through the calorie surplus you need to build muscle — the warm-up cardio before lifting already covers your conditioning base.
